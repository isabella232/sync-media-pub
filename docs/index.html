Introduction

History

The formal historical precedent for the concept of synchronized narration is the EPUB3 Media Overlays specification (digital publications with synchronized text-audio playback).

EPUB3 Media Overlays itself can be seen as the "mainstream publishing" alternative to the DAISY Digital Talking Book format, which serves the accessibility community more specifically.

Synchronized Narration is the evolution of these concepts, optimized for the open web platform and designed specifically with Web Publications in mind.

The sync-media-pub CG

The Community Group was formed to recommend the best way to synchronize media with Web Publications and other document formats being developed by the Publishing Working Group, in order to make the publications accessible to people with different types of reading requirements.

The documents below have been developed after collecting requirements and analyzing various potential solutions.
